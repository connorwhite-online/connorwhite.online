a bit about process...

It's not uncommon for me to be jumping from Figma, to VSCode, to Blender, with a file I'm converting to STL so I can send it off for 3D printing. Here's how I keep track of my work and how I begin projects.

a look at documentation

Organization is central to my workflow. Developing systems to label and file assets and iterations is just a matter of habit. Whether it's developer handoff or organizing wireframes to port to Webflow, having robust components and clear user-flows benefits efficiency and understanding.

a word on research

If I do anything, I like to push the envelop with it. There's nothing I can't stand more than a brand that doesn't stand for anything or a business that doesn't solve a problem. I'm highly intentional about my design practice, and I don't see time as a parameter that takes priority over meaningful work.

iterate, iterate, iterate

You never truly know what works until you know what doesn't. I think one of the most valuable skills one can learn is how to spot a failing idea and pivot. I strive to never be complacent with less iterations, but rather to be more efficient in prototyping.
